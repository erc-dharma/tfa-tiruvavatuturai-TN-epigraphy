Maṉṉār calāpam grant, Śaka year 1651

Metadata

Current Version:  draft, 2024-05-07Z

Editor:   Margherita Trento.

DHARMA Identifier: INSTiruvavatuturai00003

Summary: This grant records a donation by Vicaiya Rekuṉāta Cētupati Kātta Tēvar to the goddess in Tirupperuntuṟai aka Avudaiyarkoil. The donation consists in a share of the revenue of the pearl fisheries of Maṉṉār.

Translation by Margherita Trento

1Fortune! Prosperity!

1-6In the year called caumiya, which has not yet departed from the śālivakana year 1651 of the Śaka era, while the sun is moving northward, in the season of spring, on the 16th day of the month of cittirai, during the bright half, i.e., days of the waxing moon, on the auspicious day connected with the seventh day [of the lunar month], a Saturday, the day of the star anuṣa, and the planetary alignement prāha,

6-10[I], the lord of the city of Rameswaram, the one in charge of the protection of the foundation of the strait, the one in charge for worship to Śiva, the one responsible for the service to Śiva in his form as Ramanathaswamy; who is a hairy lion to his foes, one who wins over the enemies,one eminent in the sun dynasty, a sun-king, a lord with abundant pearls (or: a Ayyaṉar)

10Fortune! Prosperity!

10-45[I], the overlord of the great region, the winner over the enemy kings, the chastiser of those who commit mistakes with regard to language, or of those kings who do not keep their word, the one who rules over the three kings, i.e., Chola, Chera, Pandiyan, the one who conquers whatever country he sees, and does not give away any country he has conquered, the one who protects the offended pride, the one who sthapanacarya of the Pandimandalam, the pratishtapanacarya of Cholamandalam; the great man of the Toṇṭamaṇṭala; the king of kings who conducted elephant hunts in Ceylon, Kongu, and Jaffna, the supreme king, the Sun-king, the one who chastises evil kings,
