    Tiruvāvaṭutuṟai, Gomuktīśvara, time of Parakesarivarman, year 38

    Metadata

    Current Version:  draft, 2024-08-11Z

    Editor:   Emmanuel Francis.

    DHARMA Identifier: INSTiruvavatuturai10002

    Summary: Tax-free temple-land donation by Tirukkaṟṟaḷi Piccar.

    Hand Description:

    No distinction between e and ē, nor between o and ō.

    Medial i and ī interchangeable.

    The letter ṉ looks like ḷ.

    Translation by Emmanuel Francis

    1Prosperity! Fortune!

    1-3Thirty-eighth year of the king Parakesarivarman, who took Maturai.

    3-12The boundaries of the land, which, we, the members of the assembly of Ciṟṟāṉaiccūr, a brahmadeya in the Tiraivaḻuntūrnāṭu on the southern bank [of the Kāvēri] give, taking care of the tax money (iṟaik kāṭṭu), after receiving gold from Tirukkaṟṟaḷi Piccar of the glorious Tiruvāvaṭutuṟai, for the god of the glorious Tiruvāvaṭutuṟai [are as follows]:

    • [the eastern boundary is] west of ... ;
    • the southern boundary [is] north of ... ;
    • the western boundary [is] east of ... ;
    • the northern boundary [is] south of Iluppaiceykku.

    12-13 The land comprised between these four boundaries [is] a quarter [vēli].

    13-20 The boundaries of the land got after sale↓1 from Mutta Caṅkara Māṟāṉ [are as follows]:

    • the eastern boundary [is] west of ... ;
    • the southern boundary [is] north of ... .;
    • the western boundary [is] east of ... ;
    • the northern boundary [is] south of the Katampaṉ channel.

    20-26 The land comprised between these four boundaries, that is, ... , we, the members of the assembly of Ciṟṟāṉaiccūr, being those who give (kuṭuppōm), taking care of the tax money, as long as the moon and the sun, we have given (kuṭuttōm) [it], having engraved on stone↓2.

    26-29 From this land, ... , for the glorious drummers, I, Tirukkaṟṟaḷi Piccaṉ, have given ... , after making a binding agreement, as long as the moon and the sun.

    29 This [is under] the protection of the many Māheśvaras.
