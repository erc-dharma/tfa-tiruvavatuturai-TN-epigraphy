    Translation by Emmanuel Francis

    01[...]

    02Prosperity! Fortune!

    1-336th year of the king Parakesarivarman, who took Maturai.

    4-11 For the lord (āḷvār) [of] the glorious Tiruvāvaṭutuṟai [in] Cāttaṉūr, [which is] a brahmadeya in the Tiraimūrnāṭu on the southern bank [of the Kāvēri], [there are] a land obtained tax-free from (iṭai) the members of the assembly [of] Ciṟupuliyūr, and a land made tax-fee obtained [in] Ciṟṟāṉaiccūr [donated by] me, Tāyaṅkaṭaṉ [tāyaṅkāṭaṉ-ēṉ↓1], lord of Vīramāṅku in the Mīraikkūṟṟam.

    12-27 The boundaries of these lands, which are made tax-free ... , having obtained the gold [from me], so as to give↓2, as long as the moon, the taxes bearing upon (iṭaiyum) the members of the assembly of these two 2↓3 [villages], are [as follows]:

    • the eastern boundary is to the west of the sand dunes (maṇal-iṭu) of this god and to the land of the god of the Āditya-gṛha (ātittakaram)↓4 of this god;
    • the southern boundary is to the north of the channel of Kaṭampaṉ which he (ivaṉē↓5) has left (viṭṭa↓6) [as] boundary to the land which he obtained tax-free from the inhabitants of Ciṟṟāṉaiccūr (ciṟṟāṉaiccūrār↓7);
    • the western boundary is to the east of the land which, after purchase (vilai koṇṭu), he had dug (kalluvitta↓8), for feeding the Brahmins, in front of the god,↓9 who are the matchlessly (taṉi) expert in the (Uttara)-Sāmaveda in the Veḷḷāṟu [area] for the Lord of Tiruvāvaṭutuṟai;
    • the northern boundary is to the south of the departing (pērum?) great road, of the sand dunes (maṇal-iṭu) of the god and of the glorious flower garden [named] Civapaṭu.

    27-34 In these two 2↓10 lands, comprised within these four boundaries, [there are] sylvan tracts, dry-land, and wet-land, bordering on the south, which he had dug (kalluvitta), [as well as] dry-land and sylvan tracts, already dug (kalliṉa?↓11), on both banks of this channel ... (vāṉam) of Kaṭampaṉ ... (paḻam), already dug (kalliṉa).

    34-51 [There are] two quarters [of a vēli] of wet-land ... , bordering on the south in the land ... and comprised in these two quarters [of a vēli], a patiyam, bordering on the west, [which are provisioned as follows].

    • So that there be singing [at] two cantis [for] two singers for the Lord of Tiruvāvaṭutuṟai: six mās [are provisioned].
    • For two persons ... the glorious flower garden: four mās, west of this;
    • For the celestial(s) ... having plucked (paṟittu) glorious flower garlands ... : two mās, west of this.
    • For daily food [are provisioned]: four nāḻis of husked and seasoned paddy eighteen (?), [one] large (paruppu?) uri of lentil food-offering, one piṭi of butter food-offering, [one] uri of curd food-offering, [one] cooked food-offering, four areca-nut food-offerings. For having these food-offerings made: eight mās, east of this land that the Lord of the glorious temple obtained ...

    Commentary

    1-4Lines 1-4 are in fact layed-out in 2 columns, each with 2 lines, that is, column 1 for lines 1-2 and column 2 for lines 3-4.

    Notes

    ↑1. Syntactically, the subject of both iṟai-ili koṇṭa and iṟaiyili ceyta.
    ↑2. That is, so as to provide capital for the payment of taxes.
    ↑3. The number "2" is expressed twice, in letters and as a numeral.
    ↑4. This appears to be the present-day Cūriyaṉār Kōyil in the village of same name, in the Tanjore district, less than 10 km west of Tiruvavāṭutuṟai.
    ↑5. That is, the donor, Tāyaṅkaṭaṉ.
    ↑6. That is, dug out?
    ↑7. That is, the ūrārs of Ciṟṟāṉaiccūr?
    ↑8. That is, fitted with channels and made thus fit for cultivation?
    ↑9. This phrase possibly means that the Sāmaveda reciters are fed in front of the god.
    ↑10. The number "2" is expressed twice, in letters and as a numeral.
    ↑11. That is already fitted with channels?
