    Translation by Emmanuel Francis

    1[...]

    2Prosperity! Fortune!

    3-536th year of the king Parakesarivarman, who took Maturai.

    6-13For the lord (āḷvār) [of] the glorious Tiruvāvaṭutuṟai [in] Cāttaṉūr, [which is] a brahmadeya in the Tiraimūrnāṭu on the southern bank [of the Kāvēri], I, Tāyaṅkaṭaṉ, his slave, lord of Vīramāṅku↓1 in the Mīraikkūṟṟam, [there is] a land that [I] obtained tax-free from the members of the assembly of Ciṟupuliyūr and a land, obtained in Ciṟṟāṉaiccūr, that [I] made tax-free.

    13-29 So that these two [lands] be given tax-free, as long as the moon, from/for (iṭaiyum) the members of the assembly, having obtained gold, the boundaries to the land made tax-free [are as follows]:

    • the eastern boundary is to the west of the sand dunes (maṇal-īṭu) of this god and to the land of the god in/of the Āditya-gṛha (ātittakaram) of this god;
    • the southern boundary is to the north of ...;
    • the western boundary is to the east of ...;
    • the northern boundary is to the south of ...

    29ff.

    Commentary

    1-4Lines 1-4 are in fact layed-out in 2 columns, each with 2 lines, that is, column 1 for lines 1-2 and column 2 for lines 3-4.
